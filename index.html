Top 10 Test Knocks Of The Decade 2016-25


Aiden Markram's WTC Final Heroics: 136(207)

Aiden Markram's heroic 136-run knock came under extreme pressure in the World Test Championship Final against Australia at Lord's, leading South Africa to a historic ICC trophy win after a 27-year drought.

This match-winning century came against a formidable Australian attack in the high-stakes final of the World Test Championship 2023-25 at the iconic Lord's, London. Markram anchored South Africa's second-innings chase, displaying a blend of aggression and defensive solidity when the pressure was at its peak. His dismissal of the target was a masterclass in final-innings batting, neutralizing Australia's bowlers and systematically chipping away at the required runs. The victory secured the coveted ICC trophy for South Africa after a 27-year drought, cementing this knock as one of the most significant in the nation's cricketing history and a defining moment of Markram’s career.

Ben Stokes's Headingley Miracle: 135*(219)

Ben Stokes pulled off the "Miracle of Headingley" with an unforgettable and audacious 135 not out against Australia in the 2019 Ashes, single-handedly securing a historic one-wicket victory and saving the series for England.

Ben Stokes's heroics at Headingley during the 2019 Ashes Series are arguably the greatest fourth-innings chase in Test history. Trailing by 359 and later needing 73 runs for the last wicket, Stokes launched a breathtaking counter-attack alongside Jack Leach. He farmed the strike, smashing 11 fours and 8 sixes, taking on the likes of Hazlewood and Cummins. The sheer audacity and control he exhibited under paralyzing pressure was a display of mental fortitude few have ever matched. This innings not only led England to an improbable one-wicket victory but also kept the series alive, transforming an Ashes whitewash certainty into a memorable, competitive battle, and forever cementing the term "Headingley Hero".

Kusal Perera's Galle Heist: 153*(200)

Kusal Perera played a miraculous lone-hand innings of 153 not out against South Africa in Durban, leading Sri Lanka to an incredible one-wicket win and a historic Test series conquest in South Africa.

Facing a monumental target of 304 against a potent South African pace attack, Kusal Perera played a lone hand that redefined the concept of a tail-end partnership. His unbeaten 153 came primarily with the tail, most notably the 78-run, 10th-wicket stand with Vishwa Fernando, who contributed just 6 runs. Perera’s dazzling array of strokes and calculative risk-taking stunned the home side. The innings was a masterclass in chase management, with Perera ensuring he had the strike and dominating the scoring. This unbelievable performance led Sri Lanka to a historic one-wicket victory, making them the first Asian team to conquer South Africa in a Test series in their own backyard, a truly monumental achievement

Cheteshwar Pujara's Melbourne Blockathon: 193(373)

Cheteshwar Pujara's career-defining marathon knock of 193 in the 2018 Boxing Day Test in Melbourne anchored India's batting and paved the way for their first-ever series victory in Australia.

Pujara's marathon knock in the Boxing Day Test of the 2018-19 Border Gavaskar Trophy in Melbourne was the foundation upon which India built their historic series win. His innings was a demonstration of immense patience, unparalleled concentration, and an almost impenetrable defense, neutralizing Australia's bowling attack over two full days. Pujara’s ability to wear down the opposition bowlers allowed the rest of the Indian lineup to play with greater freedom. This knock was instrumental in India setting a massive total, securing an emphatic victory, and ultimately leading India to become the first-ever Asian team to win a Test series in Australia, a feat that demanded resilience and technical excellence.

Rishabh Pant's Gabba Assault: 89*(138)

Rishabh Pant's fearless and stunning 89 not out in the final Test at the Gabba broke Australia's 32-year fortress streak and sealed one of India's most heroic Border-Gavaskar Trophy series victories.

The final day of the 2020-21 Border Gavaskar Series at the Gabba, Brisbane, saw one of India's most audacious and successful chases. With the series on the line, Rishabh Pant played a fearless, match-winning innings, shattering Australia's 32-year unbeaten streak at their 'fortress.' Against the likes of Lyon, Cummins, and Hazlewood, Pant displayed incredible maturity, combining aggressive strokeplay with sensible defense when required. His unbeaten 89 ensured India reached the target of 328 on a deteriorating fifth-day pitch. This high-octane knock was the culmination of a truly remarkable series comeback for a severely injury-hit Indian side, symbolizing a shift in power and cricketing courage.

Virat Kohli's Edgebaston Masterpiece: 149(225)

Virat Kohli’s lone-wolf masterpiece of 149 runs against England in the 2018 Edgbaston Test was a brilliant demonstration of batting dominance that defined his 'King' status, despite the narrow loss.

Although it came in a losing cause, Virat Kohli's 149 against England in the first Test of the 2018 series at Edgbaston is universally celebrated as a masterpiece. On a challenging pitch where every other Indian batsman struggled against the swinging ball, Kohli carried the team on his back, scoring nearly 50% of the team's total in the first innings. His superb defense, calculated aggression, and ability to shepherd the tail were a demonstration of complete batting mastery in difficult conditions, erasing the memory of his previous struggles in England. This innings defined him as a player who could conquer any condition or opponent, earning him the epithet 'King' for his single-handed defiance against a world-class bowling attack.

Travis Head's WTC Final Domination: 163(174)

Travis Head's blistering 163 off just 174 balls against India in the 2023 WTC Final at The Oval seized the momentum for Australia and set up their definitive victory in the championship match.

This destructive century was played under immense pressure in the opening innings of the 2023 World Test Championship Final at The Oval in London. Australia was teetering at 76/3 when Head arrived, but he launched an incredible counter-attack, putting the Indian bowlers immediately on the back foot. He combined beautifully with Steve Smith, attacking the quicks and spinners alike with a fearless approach that swiftly took the game away from India. Head's rapid scoring rate and aggressive stroke play—including 25 boundaries and a huge six—was instrumental in lifting Australia to a commanding first-innings total of 469, earning him the Player of the Match award and delivering Australia the inaugural World Test Championship title.

David Warner's Record Triple Century: 335*(418)

David Warner hammered an undefeated 335 against Pakistan in the 2019-20 pink ball Test at Adelaide, achieving the highest individual score against a full nation team in the 2016-25 decade.

David Warner's massive, undefeated triple century against Pakistan in the 2019-20 pink ball Test at the Adelaide Oval was a historic display of offensive batting. This innings demonstrated Warner's ability to combine patient buildup with explosive power, dominating the Pakistan bowlers for almost two full days. At the time of declaration, his 335* became the highest individual score against a full nation team in the decade 2016-25, surpassing Mark Taylor and sitting behind only Matthew Hayden for the highest Australian Test score. It was a flawless display of sustained aggression, providing the perfect platform for an innings victory and illustrating his capacity for world-record-breaking performances.

Steven Smith's Redemption Century: 144(219)

Steven Smith marked his brilliant and defiant comeback after a one-year ban with a courageous 144 against England in the opening Test of the 2019 Ashes, rescuing Australia from a disastrous collapse.

After a year-long ban due to the sandpaper incident, Steven Smith’s first Test innings back for Australia in the 2019 Ashes at Edgbaston was a masterclass in resilience and redemption. Walking in with Australia in deep trouble at 122/8, Smith crafted a magnificent, almost solo effort of 144 to take the team to a respectable total. He displayed phenomenal grit, blocking out the hostile crowd and the intense pressure of his comeback. This innings of sheer will not only rescued Australia from a disastrous position but also powered them to a match victory, signaling that the 'Sandpaper Gate' controversy had not diminished his extraordinary batting talent. It was the perfect, emphatic return to the world stage.

KL Rahul's Centurion Fortress Breach: 129(260)

KL Rahul's patient and masterful 129 at Centurion was the cornerstone of India's batting effort, leading them to their first-ever Boxing Day Test victory at South Africa's formidable fortress in 2021.

KL Rahul’s exceptional century in the Boxing Day Test of 2021 against South Africa at Centurion was a triumph of technique and concentration on a pitch renowned for its difficult, quick, and uneven bounce. As an opener, Rahul set the tone for the entire series, building a massive first-innings platform of 327, of which he scored a crucial 129. His application and discipline were remarkable, seeing off the new ball threat from Rabada, Ngidi, and Jansen. This dominant innings allowed India to control the match from the start, ultimately leading to their first-ever Test victory at South Africa's formidable Centurion fortress, a venue where the hosts rarely lose, making the win a historic achievement for the team.
