# Greatness In The Whites  
### Top 10 Test Knocks (2016–25)

---

## Aiden Markram – 136 (207), WTC Final 2023
- Venue: Lord’s, London  
- Opponent: Australia  
- Significance: Anchored South Africa’s chase to end a 27-year ICC trophy drought.  
- Style: Calm under pressure, blending defense with aggression.  

---

## Ben Stokes – 135* (219), Ashes 2019
- Venue: Headingley  
- Situation: England chasing 359, last wicket stand needing 73.  
- Partner: Jack Leach (1 run contribution).  
- Impact: One-wicket win, immortalized as the “Headingley Miracle.”  

---

## Kusal Perera – 153* (200), Durban 2019
- Target: 304 vs South Africa’s pace attack.  
- Partnership: 78-run 10th wicket stand with Vishwa Fernando (6 runs).  
- Impact: Historic one-wicket win, first Asian team to win a Test series in SA.  

---

## Cheteshwar Pujara – 193 (373), Melbourne 2018
- Match: Boxing Day Test, Border-Gavaskar Trophy.  
- Style: Marathon patience and defense across two days.  
- Impact: Laid foundation for India’s first-ever Test series win in Australia.  

---

## Rishabh Pant – 89* (138), Gabba 2021
- Target: 328 on Day 5 pitch.  
- Opponent: Australia (Lyon, Cummins, Hazlewood).  
- Impact: Fearless chase broke Australia’s 32-year Gabba fortress streak.  

---

## Virat Kohli – 149 (225), Edgbaston 2018
- Context: Lone resistance on swinging pitch.  
- Contribution: Nearly half of India’s runs in first innings.  
- Impact: Defined Kohli’s “King” status despite narrow defeat.  

---

## Travis Head – 163 (174), WTC Final 2023
- Venue: The Oval, London.  
- Situation: Australia 76/3 when he arrived.  
- Style: Counter-attack with 25 boundaries and a six.  
- Impact: Player of the Match, secured WTC title vs India.  

---

## David Warner – 335* (418), Adelaide 2019
- Match: Pink Ball Test vs Pakistan.  
- Achievement: Highest individual score of the decade (2016–25).  
- Style: Patient buildup combined with explosive power.  

---

## Steven Smith – 144 (219), Ashes 2019
- Context: First Test after one-year ban.  
- Situation: Australia collapsed to 122/8.  
- Impact: Redemption century, rescued Australia, marked emphatic comeback.  

---

## KL Rahul – 129 (260), Centurion 2021
- Match: Boxing Day Test vs South Africa.  
- Pitch: Quick, uneven bounce.  
- Impact: Anchored India’s first-ever win at Centurion fortress.
